Oral Hygiene

Get It Done Or It Will
Be Too Late!

Home Blog Article

Teeth are an integral part of the body. It is very important to take care of your teeth to prevent any damage.

A tooth is covered by a natural protective layer called ‘ENAMEL’. Enamel is the hardest tissue in the human body, but once it gets destroyed, it cannot be regenerated. It gets damaged permanently. Therefore, it is absolutely vital to get any damage treated before it spreads deeper into the inner tissues of the tooth i.e. the DENTIN and the PULP.

"Early management of a damaged tooth is highly beneficial. Neglecting minor decay can lead to the infection progressing deep into the root tissues, causing severe pain and structural loss."

Preventive Dental Fillings

One should check their teeth in the mirror regularly for any black spots or decay. As soon as you notice any discoloration or black spots on your teeth, visit your dentist immediately.

If a tooth is initially decayed, it can be treated quickly and painlessly by filling the decayed cavity with a modern tooth-colored composite filling material. This is the right time to get it filled!

If a black spot or cavity is neglected, the decay progresses deeper, damaging the inner tissues of your teeth. This leads to pain and sensitivity to hot and cold food items.

Neglecting decay can eventually lead to complete tooth destruction. Get your tooth filled while the decay is superficial, before it starts giving you pain. Once the tooth starts paining, simple fillings will no longer suffice, and more advanced treatments will be required.

Root Canal Treatment (RCT)

Once decay reaches the pulp (nerve tissue), a Root Canal Treatment (RCT) must be performed to save the tooth and get rid of the pain.

Modern RCT is a painless procedure performed under local anesthesia. It typically requires 3 to 4 clinical settings, after which a dental crown is placed to restore the original tooth form and strength. This is the right time to get treated by RCT!

Neglecting tooth pain by simply taking temporary painkillers does not cure the issue; it only masks the pain while the underlying infection spreads in the bone. Further negligence makes the tooth weak, prone to fractures, and eventually untreatable—ultimately leading to extraction.

"Get your tooth treated before it is too late to save it!"
